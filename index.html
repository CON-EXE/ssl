Web Security

What is SSL?

SSL, or Secure Sockets Layer, is like a digital security guard for websites. It creates a secure connection between your web browser and the website, making sure any information you send (like passwords or credit card numbers) is encrypted and protected from eavesdroppers.

What is TLS?

TLS, or Transport Layer Security, is like an upgraded version of SSL. It serves the same purpose: protecting the information you send over the internet by encrypting it so that only the intended recipient can read it. This helps keep your data safe from hackers or anyone trying to eavesdrop.

How can you tell if a web page is secure?

To check if a webpage is secure, look for a padlock icon in the address bar of your web browser. This icon indicates that the website is using SSL or TLS to encrypt your data. Also, the website address should start with "https://" instead of just "http://". The "s" stands for "secure," meaning your connection to the site is encrypted and safer from potential eavesdroppers.

What does it mean to be secure?

In terms of web security, being secure means protecting your data and online activities from threats like hackers, malware, and unauthorized access. A secure website ensures that any information you share, such as passwords or credit card details, is encrypted so that it can't be easily intercepted or tampered with.

Why are SSL and TLS important?

SSL and TLS are vital for web security because they protect your data during transmission. They encrypt the information exchanged between your device and the website's server, making it difficult for hackers to intercept or tamper with it. This encryption ensures that sensitive information like passwords, credit card details, and personal data remain confidential.

What is encryption?

Encryption is like a secret code for your data. It transforms readable information, like text or numbers, into a scrambled, unreadable format. Only someone with the right "key" can decode it and understand the original message. This process keeps your sensitive information, such as passwords and credit card numbers, safe from unauthorized access. It's widely used in online communications, banking, and many other areas to ensure that your data stays private and secure, even if it gets intercepted by hackers.

What is an SSL certificate authority?

An SSL Certificate Authority (CA) is like a trusted digital notary. When a website wants to prove it's secure and safe to visit, it gets an SSL certificate from a CA. The CA checks the website's identity to make sure it's legitimate. Once the website passes this check, the CA issues a certificate. This certificate lets the website create secure connections with visitors, keeping their data private and protected from hackers. In short, the CA verifies the website's identity and helps keep your online interactions safe.
